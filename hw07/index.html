Reading Response: I notice we didnt get assigned a reading response this week, but I had already picked some because I thought there would be. And there were cool articles in the regular news, related to programming. I figured if I already read them I might as well re-cap them anyways too.
The first was a new twitterbot somebody made that inputs Trump's tweets into the official paperwork style of the official Oval Office Press Releases: https://www.nytimes.com/2017/10/15/business/media/trump-twitter-bots.html
The story goes that a former White House staffer (under Obama) jokingly posted a single tweet of Trumps in the Official style. Then a random programmer saw it and decided to make a bot do that to ALL of Trump's tweets. Even more inspiring is that he threw the bot together in an hour! It doesn't look as good as the White House staffer's original, but he built on the idea, and that collaborative power of the internet is pretty swift and amazing. I even think I understand how that code might be written. The next hurdle (which is huge) is actually just noticing those moments, and deciding to make the thing.

The second was more of a wormhole thru a few articles. This hero vigilante programmer guy, Marcus Hutchins, notoriously saved England early this year from a crazy North Korean Ransomware called WannaCry that was ransoming all kinds of infrastructure and hospitals. He apparently looked at an excerpt of the virus's code, saw that parts were pointing to a URL that wasnt registered, registered it himself not knowing what that might do. Turned out to be the off-switch, so he sorta accidently saved the UK. Well, just this week while he was in America for a DefCon Security Conference, he suddenly got arrested out of nowhere by the FBI, -- for alledgedly creating/distributing the Kronos virus, 3 years ago in 2014!
